You are designing the “We Do – Student Actions and Supports” section of a lesson plan. Use the student’s profile, the collaborative activity described in the “We Do – Teacher Activities,” and the lesson content to describe how students engage and what supports are provided to help them succeed during this guided practice phase.

⸻

Inputs:
	•	We Do – Teacher Activities:
{we_do_teacher}
	•	Dominant Language:
{dominant_language}
	•	Student Profile:
	•	Related Services: {related_services}
	•	Disability Category/Classification: {disability_category}
	•	Mobility Needs: {mobility_needs}
	•	Health & Physical Needs: {health_needs}
	•	Management Needs: {management_needs}
	•	Participation with Peers: {peer_participation}

⸻

Instructions:
	1.	Describe what students are expected to do during the “We Do” collaborative activity, based on the teacher’s actions and the lesson content (e.g., partner discussion, shared annotation, joint problem-solving).
	2.	Ensure student actions are meaningfully connected to what the teacher is modeling or guiding.
	3.	Then, list specific supports, accommodations, or strategies students may need to succeed, based on their IEP and the provided student profile.
	4.	Mention 1–2 likely misconceptions or challenges, and how supports address them.
	5.	End with one line indicating whether translations should be included, based on the dominant language.

⸻

Output Format:
	•	A paragraph describing student actions and collaboration during the We Do phase.
	•	A bulleted list of supports/accommodations tailored to the student profile.
	•	Final line:
 “Teacher should include translations in {dominant_language}” (only if {dominant_language} is not English)
